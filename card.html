Penguin

All penguin species live in the southern hemisphere, from the Galapagos Islands to the Antarctic continent. None in the wild lives in the North Pole.

  • Species: 17 penguin species
  • Habitat: cold waters, blocks of ice, tropical beaches, and rocky coasts
  • Feeding: fish, krill, and squid
  • Distribution: Galapagos Islands, Antarctica, New Zealand, Australia, Chile, Argentina, South Africa, Antartica

The penguin's body is adapted for swimming. Its body is fusiform (tapered at both ends) and streamlined. A penguin has a large head, short neck, and elongated body.
